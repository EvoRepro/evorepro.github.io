WP2: Co-function and co-expression network analysis

Related organisms are similar due to comparable genomic inventories. Recent advances in comparative transcriptomics have shown that these similarities are also reflected in conserved expression profiles of genes, which can be seen in highly similar transcriptional co-regulation (co-expression) networks (Movahedi et al., 2012).

Here expression data (publicly available and data generated by EvoRepro members) will be used to generate such co-expression networks in various species. (Mutwil et al., 2011). Furthermore PlaNet, an online browser for gene expression profiles and co-expression networks, will be extended to study gene expression, co-expression networks and their conservation across species along with functional annotation. This will allow identification of conserved gene sets relevant for gamete development in lower and higher plants.
