WP5: Chromatin reprogramming and epigenetic inheritance

Genome activity relies on regulations by chromatin and its modifications including DNA methylation and histone marks. Reprogramming of these modifications takes place during gametogenesis and fertilization in animals. Only recently has work in Arabidopsis provided support for chromatin reprogramming in plants. However, its significance remains unclear and its evolutionary origin is unknown.

Here, we seek to establish the methylome in gametes of flowering plants and bryophytes, Physcomitrella and Marchantia and we will identify the genes responsible for DNA methylation establishment and maintenance.

DNA methylation targets primarily silencing of transposons while gene silencing is mediated by the epigenetic modification H3K27me3 deposition by Polycomb Repressive Complex 2. We will establish chromatin profiling in Arabidopsis male gametes to explore the role of H3K27me3 in transcriptome reprogramming. To study chromatin in Marchantia, we need to re-assemble the genome into whole chromosome and establish chromatin profiling technology in this species towards the achieve studying chromatin in the context of sexual reproduction.
